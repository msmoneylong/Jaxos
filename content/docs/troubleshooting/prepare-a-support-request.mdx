# Prepare a Support Request

Clear details help the support team understand what you saw and what you expected. Before you contact support, check the account email, current workspace or client context, and visible messages or status.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step>
    Identify the account email and workspace or client context involved.

    **Expected result:** You can describe which account and context were affected.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Note the affected page or feature, what you expected, and what occurred.

    **Expected result:** You have a clear description of the issue.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Record the steps you took and the approximate date and time.

    **Expected result:** You can describe how and when the issue appeared.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Copy any visible message or status and capture a safe screenshot if useful.

    **Expected result:** You have customer-visible details that can help explain the issue.
  </Step>
  <Step>
    Include a purchase or transaction reference only when it is relevant and safe to share.

    **Expected result:** Your request includes useful context without unnecessary sensitive information.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warn">
Never send passwords, sign-in codes, full payment card details, secret keys or tokens, private client information unrelated to the issue, or medical, financial, or identity documents unless an approved secure process specifically requests them.
</Callout>

## Make screenshots safe

Before sharing a screenshot, remove or cover email addresses, payment information, private client details, and any other unrelated personal information. Include only the relevant page, message, or status.
